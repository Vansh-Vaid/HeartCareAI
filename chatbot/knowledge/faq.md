# HeartCare AI — Frequently Asked Questions

## About the Tool

**Q: What is HeartCare AI?**
A: HeartCare AI is a friendly digital assistant that helps you and your doctor understand your heart health. It looks at 13 different pieces of health information (like your age and test results) and uses 4 AI "experts" to guess how likely you are to have heart issues. It's a guide to help you know if you should see a specialist—it's not a final medical answer.

**Q: Is this a final diagnosis?**
A: No. Think of HeartCare AI as a very smart screening tool. If it says "High Risk," it just means it's time to go see a cardiologist (a heart doctor) for a real check-up. Only a human doctor can give you a formal diagnosis.

**Q: Who should use this tool?**
A: It's designed for anyone who has their clinical test results (like blood work or an ECG) and wants to understand what they mean. It's great for patients who want to be more informed and for medical students learning the ropes.

**Q: How accurate is it?**
A: Our AI is very reliable! It correctly spots heart issues about 99% of the time. We've designed it to be extra cautious—it's much better to have a "false alarm" and get checked out than to miss a real problem.

**Q: Why is my result different than I expected?**
A: The AI looks at the "big picture." Even if one thing (like cholesterol) is normal, other patterns (like how your heart reacts to exercise) might show a risk. It's the combination of all 13 clues that gives the final answer.

## About the Health Tests

**Q: What is Chest Pain Type?**
A: This describes how you feel. "Typical" pain is that classic heavy feeling in the chest. "Asymptomatic" means you don't feel any pain at all—which is actually very important to track because heart issues can sometimes be "silent."

**Q: What is a normal blood pressure?**
A: A healthy reading is usually below 120. If it's over 140, your heart is working a bit too hard. Keeping your blood pressure steady helps protect your heart over time.

**Q: What is a good cholesterol level?**
A: Aiming for below 200 is ideal! If it's over 240, it's considered "High Risk." Think of cholesterol as stuff that can slowly clog the "pipes" to your heart.

**Q: What is Max Heart Rate (thalachh)?**
A: This is the fastest your heart beats during an exercise test. If your heart can't reach a healthy speed when you're working hard, it might be a sign it needs a bit more support.

**Q: What is ST Depression (oldpeak)?**
A: This is a fancy term for a measure of heart stress during exercise. Usually, it should be 0. If it's higher than 1.0, it's a sign your heart might be struggling a little under pressure.

**Q: What is Thalassemia (thal)?**
A: In our app, this refers to a special heart scan. "Normal" means blood is flowing perfectly. Other results might mean some areas of the heart aren't getting quite as much blood as they need.

**Q: What is Exercise-Induced Angina (exang)?**
A: This simply means: "Did you feel chest pain while exercising?" If yes, it's a very important clue that your heart wants more oxygen when you're active.

## About Your Result

**Q: What does "High Risk" mean?**
A: It means the AI thinks your patterns look like someone who might have heart disease. **Don't panic!** It's just a signal to book an appointment with a heart doctor to get a professional opinion and some follow-up tests.

**Q: What does "Low Risk" mean?**
A: It means the AI didn't see any immediate signs of trouble. It's a good result, but remember to still listen to your body. If you feel unwell, always see a doctor regardless of what the AI says.

**Q: What does the percentage mean?**
A: It's our AI's way of saying how confident it is. A 75% score means the AI is quite sure this pattern looks like heart disease. It helps your doctor know how urgent your follow-up should be.

**Q: What should I do if I get a High Risk result?**
A: 1) Stay calm—it's just a screening! 2) Call your doctor and show them your report. 3) Ask about follow-up tests like a heart scan or an ultrasound. 4) Don't change any of your medicines without talking to your doctor first.

## Emergency

**Q: I have chest pain right now! What should I do?**
A: **Stop using this app and call 911 (or your local emergency number) immediately.** This app is for information, not for emergencies. If you're having trouble breathing or have sudden chest pain, seek help right away.

## Technical Details (Simplified)

**Q: How does the AI work?**
A: We use a team of 4 different AI experts. They each look at your data from a different angle and then vote. We use the combined "team effort" to give you the most accurate answer possible.

**Q: Is my data safe?**
A: Yes! Your data is stored privately in your account. We don't share it with third parties. It's just a tool to help you and your doctor manage your health better.

**Q: Can I share my report?**
A: Yes! You can download a simple PDF report after your check-up. It's a great way to show your doctor exactly what the AI found so you can have a better conversation during your visit.
